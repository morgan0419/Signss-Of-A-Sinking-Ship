ANXIETY.
Deep Sea Pressure

I’ve always strived in school. But this year, something has changed. I feel all this added pressure from my parents, peers, and teachers. I need to keep a 4.0 GPA, score a 1400 on the SAT, and keep on top of my work for 6 different classes. In middle school, I could easily handle this. Since coming to high school, I’ve worried that my best isn’t good enough. I often panic about this and try to work twice as hard. I stay up late studying and practicing so I can do well in my classes.

Due to this, I’ve developed severe anxiety. I can’t catch my breath and I go in a downward spiral. This happens every time I go to school. I get these feelings of self-doubt. I think I’m stupid and a failure and not worthy of a good college. I struggle to get through the endless amounts of work thrown at me. I feel really alone and I have even started undergoing therapy to help me.

I thought that I was alone. But in actuality, this isn’t true. Nearly 30% of teens feel the exact same way. Not all go through treatment, although it is definitely helped me find different thought processes. right now, I’m cramming for a test and still feel stressed right now. It’s hard to get through the day. I try to use the techniques that were taught to me in therapy to have a better mindset when it comes to these things. I still go through moments of frustration and defeat. It’s a struggle to cope with my anxiety and frustration sometimes.
