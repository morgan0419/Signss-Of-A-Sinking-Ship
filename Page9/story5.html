Balance

Junior year has to be the worst one yet. I have piles and piles of homework each week. I carry so many books home that my book bag ripped. I stay up each day trying to finish it all up, but it’s hard. I have an hours worth of work in all 6 of my classes. The work is extremely hard and I struggle to get through it. It’s impossible for me to balance these classes, my extracurriculars, and a social life.

Because of this, I feel an enormous amount of stress. I rarely go out and when I do, all I can think about is all the work I have to finish. It’s so hard to focus on the good weather, a friend’s party, or even family time when all I can think about is the 4 projects, 2 essays, and 3 quizzes due within the next week. My mood has definitely changed since the start of high school, but even more so since the start of junior year. Not only do I rarely go out and spend time with my friends, but I also rarely have time to do things that benefit my mental health. I used to do yoga, meditation, and play video games to relieve stress. Now those things only cause my more stress because all I can think about is the work I should be doing. I feel like if I’m not constantly working, I might as well be doing nothing at all.

This extremely busy lifestyle has had inverse effects on my mental health, emotional health, and physical health. And the simple truth is that it doesn’t have to be this way. The amount of work given to us in school is not the best way to help us learn. Imagine this: your teachers give you 5 busy-work assignments, all varying difficulties, due the next day. If you ask any student, they will tell you that they will either A) Get the answers from a classmate B) Look them up online or C) Simply not do it. Now imagine if you gave 2 meaningful assignments. Students would be much more likely to complete them on their own, as it is not as much of a workload, so it’s not as overwhelming. Schools must learn how to not overload their students with work that we won’t even do. Assignments need to be thought out carefully and given with reason. Otherwise students will always stay up until 2AM finishing meaningless work.
